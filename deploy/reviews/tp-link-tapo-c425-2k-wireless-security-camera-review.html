The Downsides: What I Wish Someone Had Told Me

No product is perfect, and the Tapo C425 has a few quirks that might be dealbreakers for specific people. First, there’s zero HomeKit support. If you’re deep in the Apple ecosystem and want everything in one app, you’ll need to look elsewhere or add a bridge like Homebridge (which is more tinkering than most folks want). The siren is another letdown. It’s advertised as a deterrent, but it sounds like an angry mosquito trapped in a tin can. Don’t expect anyone to bolt when it goes off — the two-way audio is louder and more intimidating, so I use that instead. Also, while 2K is sharp at live view, the digital zoom is noticeably softer than a 4K camera. If you’re expecting to zoom way in on a face 50 yards away and get crisp details, you’ll be disappointed. This is a wireless camera built for proximity and everyday monitoring, not cinematic long-range surveillance. Those gripes aside, none of them undercut the sheer value you’re getting for $69.99.
