The FEZIBO’s single motor lifts the desk from 28.3” up to 46.4” at about 1 inch per second—not blazing fast, but you’re not drag racing. The Downsides: Let’s Talk About Wobble and Scratches

Not gonna lie—there are a couple of things you need to know before buying. First, that wobble complaint you see in negative reviews? It’s real. At max height (46.4”), if I’m typing with my usual aggressive gusto, the monitor shakes side-to-side noticeably. It’s not a dealbreaker for me because I rarely go above 44”, and at sitting height it’s rock solid. But if you’re tall and plan to use it fully extended most of the day, you’ll feel that slight shimmy. One Amazon reviewer put it bluntly: “At max height there’s noticeable side-to-side wobble when typing. Not deal-breaking but definitely there.” Second, the desktop surface is scratch-prone. I dragged a coffee mug across it once and left a faint mark. Now I use a desk mat—problem solved. The laminate isn’t premium butcher block, but for $240, you shouldn’t expect a work of art. The 176 lbs weight capacity also means you can’t load it up like a tank. Heavy setups need apply caution. If you’re a minimalist, you’ll sail right past these quirks.
