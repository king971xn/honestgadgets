First Impressions & Setup: That HomeBase Is No Joke

I ripped open the box expecting a couple of cute little cameras and a tiny hub. Instead, I pulled out the HomeBase 3 and laughed. It’s massive—picture your WiFi router’s bigger cousin. You definitely need a dedicated spot for it near your router, not tucked behind a plant. The cameras themselves are also noticeably chunkier than a Ring or Arlo. If you want a discreet security camera, these will not blend into your siding.

Setup was pain-free, though. Plug in the HomeBase, scan a QR code, pair the camera—took maybe ten minutes. The mount is solid metal, and the whole thing feels reassuringly beefy with an IP67 rating. I stuck it under the eave, pointed it at the driveway, and crossed my fingers the 365-day battery claim wasn’t marketing fluff.

The Downsides: Big Hardware and a Laggy App

Let’s get real about the gripes, because a few things will annoy you. First, that HomeBase 3 requirement isn’t just bulky—if you live in a tiny apartment, it’s a genuine eyesore. One user complained it’s “like a WiFi router-sized box you need to place centrally,” and yep, if your house layout means the base can’t reach the camera, you’ll need an extender or creative repositioning.

The camera itself is hardly discreet. If curb appeal matters, this chunky slab will stand out. Also, the app occasionally drags—tapping to see a live view sometimes takes a solid 5+ seconds, which feels like an eternity when you’re trying to see if that’s a burglar or just a squirrel. It’s not constant, but when it happens at the wrong moment, you’ll curse.
