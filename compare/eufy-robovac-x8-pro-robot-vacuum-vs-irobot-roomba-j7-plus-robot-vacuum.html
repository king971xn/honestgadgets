You’re Torn Between Two Robot Vacuums — And I Get It

I’ve been there. You’re staring at two “smart” vacuums, both promising to free you from the chore you hate most. The Eufy RoboVac X8 Pro Robot Vacuum and the iRobot Roomba j7+ Robot Vacuum sit at similar price points, both include self-empty docks, and both promise top-tier navigation. But they couldn’t be more different where it counts — and that’s what we’re going to dig into. No fluff. Just the stuff that actually matters after the unboxing excitement wears off.

Eufy RoboVac X8 Pro Robot Vacuum Deep Dive

Where It Shines

If you have a lot of carpet and furry housemates, the X8 Pro will feel like money well spent. The twin-turbine suction pulls up debris that cheaper vacs just roll over. One owner of two shedding cats mentioned it fills the bin every single run — and that’s exactly the kind of dirt you want gone. Laser mapping is fast and accurate, creating a floor plan that rivals robots twice the price. The 180-minute battery means it can clean a whole 1,800 sq ft home on a single charge without whimpering back to base. And the self-empty station? It’s noticeably quieter than the Roomba’s dock and the replacement bags cost half as much. That’s a long-term win.

Where It Stumbles

There’s no mopping. Not even a basic drag-along pad. For $400 in 2025, that feels like Eufy left a feature on the table. If you were hoping for one robot to do both, this isn’t it. Also, the side brush wears down surprisingly fast on carpet — some users are swapping it out every six weeks. That’s an extra, ongoing cost. And customer support isn’t exactly lightning; replies can take 3–5 business days, so if your robot plays dead on a Friday, you’re waiting until the middle of next week for help.

iRobot Roomba j7+ Robot Vacuum Deep Dive

Where It Shines

The obstacle avoidance is the star here. It’s not just detecting a table leg — it’s avoiding pet waste, charging cables, and stray socks with eerie precision. The P.O.O.P. guarantee is a real thing iRobot stands behind, and for dog owners who’ve experienced the horror of a “poopocalypse,” that’s priceless. The camera-based navigation learns your home layout better over time, and the ability to name rooms and then tell Alexa “clean the kitchen” feels like living in the future. The Clean Base also empties into bags that last up to 60 days, so you genuinely forget about vacuum maintenance.

Where It Stumbles

Those replacement bags are a hidden tax. A 3-pack runs about $20, which adds up to roughly $80 a year if you’re emptying regularly. For a $500 robot, you’d hope for a more affordable consumable. And like the Eufy, there’s no mopping at all. For many, that’s a non-starter. The battery life is also half of the Eufy’s — at 90 minutes, larger homes might need a mid-cycle recharge, though it does automatically resume where it left off.

Real-World Performance: Which One Fits Your Life?

Let’s break it down by actual scenarios, because specs only tell half the story.

Homes With Wall-to-Wall Carpet

The Eufy RoboVac X8 Pro pulls ahead. That 4000 Pa suction and carpet boost mode dig deep into fibers, pulling out pet hair and dust that lighter bots miss. It also has the stamina to finish the job without recharging.

Chaotic Homes with Kids, Pets, and Toys Everywhere

The Roomba j7+ is your savior. It’ll dodge LEGOs, dog bowls, and that one random shoe your toddler left in the hallway. No more pre-cleaning before you run the vacuum — it just goes and doesn’t get stuck.

Large Open-Concept Spaces

Again, the Eufy wins here. Double the run time means it can cover more ground in one session, and the laser mapping handles wide areas without getting lost. The Roomba will get there eventually thanks to recharge-and-resume, but it’s slower.

Allergy Sufferers

This one’s a toss-up. Both self-empty into sealed bags, keeping dust contained. But the Roomba’s slightly larger onboard bin might trap more before heading home, and its HEPA-style filters are top-notch. Still, the Eufy’s stronger suction pulls more out of carpets initially.

The Hidden Costs Nobody Talks About

Both robots have ongoing expenses that reviews don’t always spotlight. For the Roomba j7+, it’s the bags and filters. You’ll burn through a bag every two months on average, and official replacements are pricey. Off-brand exist, but they can affect suction and odor control.

The Eufy X8 Pro has cheaper bags (win!) but the side brush is a weak point. It frays quickly on textured surfaces, and while a pack of spares is cheap, the annoyance of swapping it every few weeks is real. Also, Eufy’s support response time can leave you stuck with a bricked bot longer than you’d like.

Neither does mopping, so if you want that, factor in a separate mop or a combo bot like a Roborock. Don’t buy either of these expecting to throw away your Swiffer.

Final Verdict: Which Robot Vacuum Is Best For You?

It’s not about which one is “better” — it’s about which headache you want to avoid.

  • You want raw power, long battery, and value: Grab the Eufy RoboVac X8 Pro. It’s the budget-friendly workhorse that chews through mess on carpet and keeps your floors respectable without constant attention.
  • You need obstacle avoidance and a truly smart home experience: The iRobot Roomba j7+ is worth the extra hundred bucks. The peace of mind alone — no poop smears, no cord chaos — is something Eufy can’t match yet.
  • You’re on a tight long-term budget: The Eufy wins again because those cheaper bags add up to real savings. Just be ready to buy a multi-pack of side brushes and wait a bit if something goes wrong.

Both are solid choices. I’d choose the Roomba if I had young kids or an accident-prone pet; I’d choose the Eufy for a carpet-heavy home where every penny counts.
